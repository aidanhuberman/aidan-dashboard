Weekly Putting Routine

Once a week — dialing in inside 10 feet, trusting the stroke

1x / week
Warmup — feel the stroke
1
On-Line Warmup Putts
30 putts — use alignment rod or tee gate on straight putt
Ball starts on line every time. Not about the make, about the start line.
2
Left Hand Only
10 putts — one hand, feel the face
Lead hand controls the face. Feel it square through impact.
3
Right Hand Only
10 putts — trail hand, feel the speed
Right hand controls pace. Match the distance, no stabbing.
4
Towel Drill
10 putts — towel under both arms, keep connection
If towel drops, arms are breaking down. Shoulders rock, arms quiet.
Pressure — Around the World
5
Around the World — 3 to 8 Footers
Circle 6 tees around hole at 3–8 feet. Must make 4/5 twice to move on.
Goal: 4/5 completed twice
If you miss 2 in a row, restart. This is your bread and butter range — own it.
Pace Control — No Score, Just Feel
6
Distance Ladder — 12 / 24 / 36 feet
5 putts at each distance — no make/miss goal, pure pace feel
Your pace is already good. Just confirm it. Die at the hole, never 3 feet past.
Make drill — dial inside 15 feet
7
The Clock Drill — Inside 15 Feet
12 spots around hole at 6, 10, and 15 feet (4 of each). Work the clock.
Goal: 8/12 makes minimum. Log your score each week.
Pick your spot on the lip, commit, don't steer it. Inside 10 feet is non-negotiable.
Once a week, same order, same feels. By June 15 this should take 30–40 min and feel like second nature.
